Apply to become an academy privacy notice

The Apply to become an academy service is run by the Department for Education (DfE), which is also the Data Controller for any information we collect.

This notice explains how we use your data.

What data we collect

  • school headteacher details including their name, email address and phone number
  • governing body details including the name of the chair, their email address and their phone number

We also use the DfE Sign-in service to capture the name of the author of the application, as well as their role and email address.

Why we use it

We capture this data so that we can:

  • consider a school's application to become an academy
  • coordinate teams that are involved in the conversion process
  • contact you about your application
  • invite people to participate in user research
  • get feedback on the service

Who we share your data with

We sometimes need to make the information we collect from you available to our partners.

These include:

  • The Education and Skills Funding Agency
  • advisory board members

For the purpose of improving usability and accessibility, we also:

  • share data with a contracted third party
  • capture anonymised Google Analytics data

We may share your information with the police or other authorities if required by law.

How long we keep your data

We will hold your data for 6 years.

Our legal basis

The law says that we are allowed to use the data you provide to:

  • perform a public task as part of our function as a department

Your rights

By law you are entitled to:

  • ask to see a copy of any information we hold about you - this is called a Subject Access Request (SAR)
  • ask for mistakes in your data to be corrected
  • ask us to stop processing it
  • ask us to delete it

If you ask us to delete or stop processing data relating to an ongoing application, we may not be able to proceed with the conversion.

  • DfE's Personal Information Charter contains information about other data it holds and how to make an SAR.
  • The Information Commissioner's Office can tell you more about your data protection rights.

Contact us or make a complaint

If you have any questions about the way we use your data, or if you want to make a complaint, you can:

  • contact DfE directly using 'Apply to become an academy' as a reference, or
  • write to our Data Protection Officer (DPO)
